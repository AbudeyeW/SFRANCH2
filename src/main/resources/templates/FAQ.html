Address concerns regarding safety measures, required riding equipment, and guidelines for riders to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

No prior riding experience is necessary. Our trail rides are suitable for riders of all skill levels, including beginners. Our knowledgeable guides will provide basic riding instructions and ensure your safety throughout the ride.

We provide all necessary riding equipment, including saddles, stirrups, and riding helmets. If you have specific equipment preferences or requirements, feel free to let us know in advance, and we'll do our best to accommodate them.

Riders must be at least 12 years old to participate in the trail ride. Additionally, we have a weight limit of 250 pounds to ensure the safety and comfort of our horses.

We recommend wearing long pants, closed-toe shoes with a small heel, and comfortable clothing suitable for outdoor activities. Avoid loose or baggy attire that may get tangled or caught during the ride.

Yes, we have a few guidelines to ensure a safe and enjoyable ride for everyone. These may include staying on designated trails, following the instructions of the guide, maintaining a safe distance between horses, and respecting the natural environment.

Our guides are trained in first aid and carry first aid kits during the trail ride. In case of any medical emergencies, they can provide immediate assistance and coordinate further medical attention if needed.

Your safety is our priority, and we closely monitor weather conditions. In the event of inclement weather, such as heavy rain or storms, we may need to reschedule or cancel the trail ride. We will communicate any changes or updates to you in a timely manner.

We recommend keeping personal belongings to a minimum during the trail ride. Small items like cell phones, keys, or cameras can be securely stored in provided saddlebags or pouches. However, we advise against bringing valuable or fragile items that may be at risk of damage or loss during the ride.
